Case Studies

GAUTENG

Our foundation has proudly partnered with five school in the Gauteng province over the past four years. This province has the highest number of students enrolled in tutoring services and over 300 students are receiving annual funding assistance for school fees.

  • Alexandra Primary School and Soweto Secondary School: Our foundation has specifically assisted with the funding of over 100 learners in these schools for their annual school fees each year. Over 200 learners received basic stationery packs at the start of 2016.
  • Dobsonville High School: This school was awarded 'Most Improved Matriculant results with Exemption' by the Department of Education in 2016. We've seen a particular growth in the number of matriculants passing higher grade mathematics and science in this school - 21 to date! Three of our matriculant scholars that were enroled with our foundation were awarded scholarships by the Department to futher their tertiary studies.
  • Mamelodi Primary School and High School: Over 400 learners received basic stationery packs at the start of 2016. The school also received a generous donation of 200 new school desks and chairs from Pack 'n Save.
  • Isitimela High School: Situated in the north-west corner of Gauteng, Isitimela High School produces a surprising amount of mathematics and science marticulants, owing to the strongly held values of their principle Mr. Zondi to ensure good quality education is provided. Our foundation supported this effort by funding two new teachers to pursue further tertiary qualifications in 2016 to uphold the quality of education in this school.

KWA-ZULU NATAL

In 2015, our foundation expanded to include to schools from the rural Kwa-Zulu natal which has now grown to seven schools!

  • Sima Primary School, Sihamba Primary School and Sifundani Primary School: Grade 0 - 7 tutoring was provided to over 50 learners in 2016 with the majority of learners in the foundation phase. Over 400 learners received basic stationery packs at the start of 2016.
  • Valley of 1000 Hills Secondary and High School: Our foundation has specifically assisted with the funding of over 60 learners in these schools for their annual school fees each year. Over 400 learners received basic stationery packs at the start of 2016.
  • uShaka Mission School and Abanesibindi High School: These schools joined our foundation at the start of 2017 - we hope to assist with funding learners school fees and identify learners requiring tutoring for the second term this year.

WESTERN CAPE

In 2016 our foundation opened a new branch in Cape Town and has partnered with three schools in the nearby townships for the past year. We have seen the biggest improvements in our students in these schools with an overall improvement of 35% in overall marks in 2017. To date, 29 learners in the Western Cape passed (>50%) in Grade 12 mathematics and science through our foundation.

  • Khayalitsha Secondary School: 17 learners received ongoing tutoring in 2016.
  • Gugulethu High School: 10 learners received ongoing tutoring in 2016. The Hope Foundation (www.hope.org) partnered with this school to identify learners affected by fires to distribute food vouchers to their families. Our foundation replaced uniforms, stationery and books for those learners affected.
  • Ndawonye Primary School: Over 200 learners received basic stationery packs at the start of 2016. The school also received a generous donation of 200 new school desks and chairs from Pack 'n Save.
